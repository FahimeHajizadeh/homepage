About Me

I am a Ph.D. graduate in Electrical Engineering from Polytechnique Montréal, where I worked under the supervision of Prof. Jean-Pierre David and Prof. Tarek Ould-Bachir. My doctoral research focused on real-time simulation of power systems using FPGA-based Frequency Dependent Network Equivalent (FDNE) models.

I am currently a Real-Time Simulation Expert at Huawei, where I apply and expand my expertise in FPGA-based simulation and hardware acceleration for power electronics and control. Prior to this, I completed an internship at OPAL-RT Technologies in Montreal, contributing to FPGA and HLS-based power system modeling, and gained experience as an Embedded Systems Engineer.

I hold Bachelor's and Master's degrees in Computer Engineering from Iran, specializing in digital systems and approximate computing. My Master’s thesis, ranked at the top of my class, introduced an energy-efficient approximate multiplier design.

My research has resulted in several peer-reviewed publications in leading journals and conferences, and I also serve as a reviewer for high-impact journals in my field. My technical expertise includes HLS, VHDL, Verilog, C++, RT-LAB, MATLAB, and Simulink.

My research interests span offline and real-time simulation, configurable hardware systems, hardware automation, and the integration of AI-driven solutions into simulation and control.
